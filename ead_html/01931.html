Biographical/Historical note

The Norton Funeral Home manuscript collection contains the records of three generations of this family owned and operated funeral director's business in Michigan. The collection dates from 1919 - 1951. These papers reflect the business interests of members of the family run funeral homes in Detroit, Dearborn and Cheboygan, as well as, a funeral directors finance service in Detroit from 1932 -1933, and a paint and wallpaper store in Fenton from 1948 -1951. Isaac P. Norton established the business with George Engelhart in 1901, by 1905 he was the sole owner of the funeral home located at 761 Michigan Avenue in Detroit. Isaac Norton died in 1913, and the funeral home was managed by his son Albert H. Norton. It was Albert H. Norton, who expanded the family business adding homes at two other locations in Detroit, as well as, the homes in Dearborn and Cheboygan. Albert's son, Albert Maurice was the director of the Funeral Director's Finance Service, which he ran until he took over operation of the Detroit funeral home. The Funeral Director's Finance Service was a sales organization established to serve the needs of the funeral homes in Detroit. The collection contains the records of sales calls maintained by this business. (blue index cards)
