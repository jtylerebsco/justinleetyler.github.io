Abstract: The Irvin Gottschalk Papers contains correspondence to his sister, Clara Selesky, and her husband Edward from 1942-1945, newspaper clippings from 1942-1945 and photographs from 1942-1946.
Language: In English.
Repository: Detroit Public Library. Burton Historical Collection.

Biographical/Historical note

Born about 1910 in Warren, MI , Irvin Gottschalk was an American soldier during World War II. He entered these service in 1942 and was in the Philippines in 1945. It is not clear through the letters if he saw combat personally as it is never mentioned. Gottschalk mentions going to France and staying mostly in Normandy. He died in 1975.

Arrangement note

The boxes are arranged by type of document: correspondence, newspaper clippings and photographs. Many of the photographs have unidentified men in uniforms.

Other finding aids note

Finding aid and white index cards available in the Burton Historical Collection and on the World Wide Web.
