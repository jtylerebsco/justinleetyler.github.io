Biographical/Historical note

Born and raised in Portland, Mich., Alfred Ray Canfield started the Clare County Cleaver, a weekly newspaper, at Harrison, Mich. after his graduation from high school. He was later appointed postmaster of Harrison. He and Minnie Orcelia Bogue, of Harrison, married, and they had five children. In 1887, Mr. Canfield moved to Clare, where he founded the Clare Courier. In 1910, Mr. Canfield was appointed tally clerk of the House of Representatives, a position he held for nine years. He then devoted his time to the Courier. He continued as editor and publisher until 1921, when he sold the paper and moved to Detroit. During his career as a country newspaper editor, Mr. Canfield gained a reputation across Michigan. In Detroit, he became secretary of the Democratic State Central Committee, and remained in that position until his death in Sept. 1926 a the age of fifty-nine.
