Biographical/Historical note

John F. Kelly was born on 6 September 1949 to Robert Corby Kelly and Margaret Elizabeth Kelly and is the fourth of seven children. After attending St. Matthew's Elementary School and Bishop Gallagher High School, he enrolled at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, where he received an A.B. degree with honors in 1972. He also holds a Master of Public Administration degree from Wayne State University, Detroit, 1974. In addition, John holds a Juris Doctor degree from the Detroit College of Law, 1983, and was admitted to the State Bar of Michigan in 1984. He completed a Ph.D. degree in political science at Wayne State University in 1996. His dissertation is entitled An Intra National Foreign Policy Agenda for Sub National Government. John is married to Toni Marie Kelly and they have two children: Rebecca Lynn and Dana Marie. His religious affiliation is Roman Catholic. John enlisted in the United States Army Reserve in 1972 and served four years. He holds the rank of Major in the Michigan National Guard. John was elected to serve in the State Senate in 1 978 and was reelected in 1982, 1986, and 1990. He retired from the State Senate in 1994. John is active in several community, neighborhood, and civic organizations, including the Detroit Committee on Foreign Relations and Amnesty International. He is involved in veterans' activities and is a member of the American Legion. He continues to support and work against casino gambling in Detroit.

Series I, Airport Expansion and Opposition
General Note
Correspondence, reports, climatological data, lay-out and master plans, studies of noise level and compatibility, court cases, rebuttals, and other documents relating to the expansion of Detroit City Airport are found within this series. There are several folders dealing in great detail with an environmental assessment survey completed in 1987. Financing and funding issues are thoroughly examined. Various court cases in opposition of expanding municipal facilities in other cities as well as the Detroit City Airport are also included. The five court cases are from 1960 - 1985 and include cases not only in Detroit, but also Seattle, East Lansing, and Minnesota. files in Series I are alphabetically arranged by subject or by type of material.

Series II, Casino Gambling
General Note
Correspondence, speeches, promotions, economic concerns and fundamentals, media coverage, a court case against the governor, and a study commission's final report on the issue are just some of the materials included in this very important series. Senator John Kelly became personally involved in this very controversial issue and worked hard and long against allowing casino gambling in the city of Detroit. A special commission was appointed to study the issue of casino gambling. Although their final report came out in June 1988, this was, by no means, the end of the controversy. Materials on the subject are very prominent all through the early 1 990s. files in Series II are alphabetically arranged by subject or by type of material.
