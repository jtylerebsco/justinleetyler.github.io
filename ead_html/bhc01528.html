Biographical/Historical note

Augustus W. Leggett and Eliza Seaman, natives of New York City, were married there on November 23, 1836 and moved to Michigan in 1852. Mrs. Leggett was active in literary and civic circles and as a close friend of Mr. And Mrs. William Cullen Bryant. In Detroit she promoted the first woman's club and made her home a social, musical and literary center. Mr. And Mrs. Leggett had three children, one of whom. Perceval S., served as lieutenant in the 5th Michigan Cavalry during the Civil War. He was killed in Virginia in September 1863. Some of his letters and those of other soldiers, friends or relatives of the family are included.
