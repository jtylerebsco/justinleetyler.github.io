Biographical/Historical note

Includes manuscript of Alger's book, The Spanish American War and papers on the Boer War.

Biographical/Historical note

Russell A. Alger was governor of Michigan, 1885-1887; U.S. Secretary of War, 1897-1899; and U.S. Senator, 1902-1907. He also engaged in the lumber industry.Born in Lafayette, Ohio, Feb.27, 1836; began studying law in Akron, Ohio with Wolcott andamp; Upson, 1857; removed to Grand Rapids, Mich., 1858 andamp; began industrial work; entered military service andamp; had part in 66 battles andamp; skirmishes, 1861-65; brevet, Major General; settled in Detroit, 1866; republican representative in convention in Chicago, 1884; was presidential candidate, 1888 and 1892; Secretary of war, 1897-99; U.S. Senator, 1903-07; died in Washington, D C. Jan. 24,1907. (from Biog. Congr. directory; Burton, Hist. of Wayne Co., v.3, p.5, with portrait) (blue index cards)
