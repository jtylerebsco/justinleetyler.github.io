Biographical/Historical note

Historic Little Rock Baptist Church was established in April of 1936 under the pastorate of the Reverend Robert E. Tate. The first services were held in the home of Ozzie and Theodosia Hooks at 1314 East Willis. The congregation later relocated to Russell Avenue. In subsequent years, the church relocated to Mack Avenue, followed by a move to Maxwell Street in 1969. In 1979, the church moved to 9000 Woodward Avenue, its present location. This neo-Gothic cathedral had formerly housed the Central Woodward Christian Church. Historic Little Rock Baptist Church continues to be a leader in religious activism and community service. Among its many outreach activities are a jail ministry, Bible Institute, radio broadcasts, bakery, and the "Shoes for Children" annual shoe drive.
