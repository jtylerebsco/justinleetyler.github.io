Biographical/Historical note

The National Association of Parliamentarians (N.A.P.) was founded in 1930, to help organizations of all sizes conduct business efficiently through education and advocacy of effective meeting procedures and the accreditation of meeting professionals. The Detroit Unit, also known as the Emma A. Fox Unit. was created to study, teach, promote and disseminate the philosophy and principles underlying the rules of parliamentary procedure, and to encourage and strengthen the parliamentary activities of its members. The Macomb Parliamentary Unit (MPU) was organized in 1987, with their first meeting held on July 21 at the Utica Presbyterian Church.
