Biographical/Historical note

Son of John and Margaret (Campbell) McArthur, born in Dutchess Co., N.Y., January 14, 1772. In 1780 he removed to Pittsburgh; took part in Harmar's campaign of 1790; and from 1792-1793 was a salt-boiler near Maysville, Ky. He joined a surveying party under Nathaniel Massie, and served as a ranger during Indian raids. In February, 1796, he married Nancy McDonald and settled on a farm near Chillicothe. By 1804 he was considered the wealthiest land holder in the Scioto Valley. (blue index cards)

Scope and Contents note

Papers deal with military activities in the northwest area in the War of 1812.
