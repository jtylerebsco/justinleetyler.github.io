Biographical/Historical note

Early automotive pioneer who worked with Charles B. King and Henry Ford. Established the Barthel Motor Company which dissolved after one year. Worked as a designer for the Oldsmobile Motor Works where he designed and built a six-cylinder speedboat engine. Invented the taper automobile frame and was a pioneer in auto interior design. Also invented a lamp for use in chromotherapy treatment of diseases. Involved in engineering and relief work for various city of Detroit projects. Credited with 34 patents.

SERIES III:L Personal Files, 1868-1955. (predominantly, 1920-1955).
Scope and Contents note
Correspondence, clippings, drafts, publications and notes relating generally to the early development of the automobile industry in Detroit, Michigan with some emphasis on the role played by Oliver Barthel, Charles B. King and Henry Ford. Included is an oral history of Barthel and a written history on the automobile industry which he attempted to have published. These were part of Barthel's numerous efforts to document an accurate history of the early automobile industry.
