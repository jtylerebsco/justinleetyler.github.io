Biographical/Historical note

The Johnson Club of Detroit was the organization that first was known as the alumni association for Johnson's automobile design school.

Andrew F. Johnson was born in Westfield, Queens County, Nova Scotia in October of 1854. He came to Gray, Maine in 1870 and was employed by a carriage and sleigh manufacturer, Smith and Leslie Company. It was here that he met and married his wife, Agnes Leslie. Johnson was trained at the Technical School for Carriage Draftsmen and Mechanics in New York City from 1883-1885. The National Carriage Builder's Association sent him to Paris to study carriage design under Mssr. Dupont from June-October of 1885. Later on in 1892 became the principal of the Technical School for Carriage and Automobile Draftsmen and Mechanics, in New York City. He would serve in this position until 1918, when he purchased the school himself. In 1920, Johnson was brought to Detroit by the Fischer Brothers to teach automobile design at the Cass Technical High School. From 1918 until his death in 1943, Johnson operated the Correspondence School for Automobile Body Makers, Designers and Draftsmen and for Carriage and Wagon Mechanics from his home in Cray, Maine. From this correspondence school, Johnson trained over 2,300 people. Johnson's students became leaders of the industry. Some of them were Charles W. Nash, William C. Durant, Frederick A. Fisher, Edward F. Fisher, Alfred J. Fisher, Otto Graebner, Carl B. Parsons, and Raymond Dietrich. Johnson was intrumental in founding the Alumni Association for graduates of the Technical School for Automobile Body Designers and Engineers in 1904. He was made a lifetime member of the Automotive Engineer's Association and the Society of Automotive Engineers. Johnson was known as the "father of automobile body design" and as the "country's first great teacher of design". On Nov. 6, 1939, Otto Graebner, Chief Engineer of the Murray Corp. was quoted in the Detroit Free Press as saying, "You might say that he designed every automobile body on the street, because we all learned the business from him." Johnson died on Dec. 23, 1943 in Gray, Maine.

Chronological list
Date Event
1854 - Andrew F. Johnson born October 17/18 in Westfield, Queens County, Nova Scotia, Canada.
1870 - Johnson came to Cray, Maine and worked for Smith and Leslie Company of West Gray, Maine. Smith and Leslie Co. manufactured carriages and sleighs. Johnson married Agnes Leslie of Gray, Maine.
1883-1885 - Johnson attended Technical School for Carriage Draftsmen and Mechanics in New York City.
1885 - Johnson is sent by National Carriage Builder's Association to Paris to study carriage design under Mssr. Dupont, June-October.
1892-1917 - Johnson serves as Principal of Technical School for Carriage and Automobile Draftsmen and Mechanics, New York City.
1904 - The Alumni Association for Technical School graduates is founded, with Johnson as first President.
1918 - Johnson purchases Technical School.
1920 - Johnson is employed as Automobile Design Teacher at Cass Technical High School, Detroit, Michigan.
1922 - Alumni Association name is changed to Alumni Association of the Technical School for Automobile Body Designers and Engineers.
1918-1943 - Johnson operated Correspondence School for Automobile Body Makers, Designers and Draftsmen and for Carriage and Wagon Mechanics from his home in Gray, Maine. He trained over 2,300 persons.
1939 - Johnson is awarded Honorary Lifetime Membership in the Society of Automotive Engineers, Nov. 6.
1943 - Johnson died at his Gray, Maine home, Dec. 23.
