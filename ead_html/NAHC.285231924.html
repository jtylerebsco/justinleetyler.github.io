Biographical/Historical note

Located on W. Fort Street in Detroit, this was the first automatic, conveyor-style car wash in the world, founded by Paul Maraian in 1946. The machine could wash one car every two minutes, and could handle up to 750 cars in one day. By 1954 that number jumped to nearly 2,000. After Paul Maraian died in 1969, his family kept the business open and renovated it to keep up with the times. by the 1980's the business had become computerized, and more efficient dryers and new steam-cleaning brushes were installed. In the early 1990s, however, the Maraian family sold the business and it became known as Hollywood Car Wash. It closed in 1994 and the building was torn down soon afterwords.

Scope and Contents note

Papers contain accounts, correspondence, photographs and newspaper articles about this Detroit company.
