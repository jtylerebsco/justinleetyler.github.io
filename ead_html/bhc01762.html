Biographical/Historical note

Born In Detroit, Feb. 24, 1888, grad. with BS in Engineering from U. of M. in 1909, became analytical chemist at Parke Davis, became Supt. of manufacturing at Walkerville, Ont. in 1919, Supt. of manufacturing in Detroit 1929, director of Scientific Relations in 1953. Retired in 1956. Named "First Citizen of Detroit" in 1957. Director of many civic organizations, in demand as a speaker, Trustee of Burton Endowment Fund, Alma College, Detroit Historical Society, Commissioner Detroit Public Library, active in many other civic and scientific organizations. Married to Buda and had son, Henry M. and daughter, Mrs. R. H. Sell, Died May 4, 1970. (from BHC biography file) (blue index cards)

Scope and Contents note

Letters, speeches and subject files reflecting his interest in scientific and civic matters.
