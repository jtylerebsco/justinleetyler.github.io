General Physical Description note (6 boxes, 1 volume, 1 extra large manuscript)
Abstract This collection focuses primarily on William and Clara Knudsen and Clara and Robert VanderKloot and their family. It contains correspondence, speeches, newspaper clippings and photographs of both the Knudsen and VanderKloot families, as well as materials from Denmark.

Biographical/Historical note

William S. Knudsen was born in Copenhagen, Denmark on March 25, 1879, coming to America twenty years later. He met Clara Euler in Buffalo, New York. The daughter of Charles and Christina Euler, tailors from Germany, Clara had been born in New York around 1880. William and Clara were married on November 1, 1911 and had four children. Their oldest daughter, Clara "Tuttie" Knudsen, married Robert VanderKloot in 1936.William and Clara Knudsen had four children: Semon "Bunkie", Clara "Tuttie", Ella "Dottie", and Martha "Girlie". The family came to Detroit from New York in 1913, where Ella and Martha were born, and that year Mr. Knudsen became a naturalized U.S. citizen. They lived in Highland Park, with a summer home on Grosse Ile. The Knudsen's were very civic-minded. Mr. Knudsen was the honorary chairman of the Queen Alexandrine Unit of the American Red Cross. He was awarded the King Christian medal for his work by the Danish Red Cross in 1948, shortly before his death. A fund in Mrs. Knudsen's honor, the Clara Elizabeth Fund, was established at Flint's Hurley Hospital to teach new mothers how to care for themselves and their children. The Lutheran School for the Deaf in Detroit was dedicated to Mrs. Knudsen, and the Knudsen Home for Children in Denmark was named for Mr. Knudsen. He was also instrumental in building two of Detroit's Lutheran churches, Epiphany and Outer Drive Faith. Clara Euler Knudsen died in Detroit on April 17, 1950 at the age of 69, almost exactly two years after the death of her husband. The Knudsen's son Semon, nicknamed `Bunkie", worked for General Motors until 1968, when he quit to become president of Ford Motor Company. Unfortunately, he was fired just nineteen months later, to be succeeded by Lee Iacocca. After leaving Ford, Bunkie went into the manufacturing of motor homes in Farmington Hills. In 1971 he became chairman and CEO of White Motor Company, which made heavy trucks and farm equipment. He retired in 1980 and died in 1998 at the age of 85. Clara Knudsen was married to Robert VanderKloot on June 16, 1936. The son of Matthew and Elizabeth VanderKloot, Robert was born in Michigan in 1914. His father was the well-to-do owner of a Detroit photo engraving company. Robert served in the Air Corps during World War II, receiving a promotion from First Lieutenant to Captain before the war's end. He became president of VanderKloot Press, Inc., a book and commercial printing firm in Detroit. Clara "Tuttie" Knudsen VanderKloot was chairman of the International Travelers Market, a benefit for the Save the Children Federation, in 1959. She also served as a national sponsor of the organization. She died at age 87 on January 9, 2001. One of Bob and Tuttie's children was Karen VanderKloot DiChiera, who served for a time as a Detroit Public Library commissioner.
