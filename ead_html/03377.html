Biographical/Historical note

Frances Davis McTeer was born on 13 January 1907 in Monett, Missouri and died on 4 January 1985 in Holliday, Florida. She was buried in the Magnolia Cemetery in Maryville, Tennessee. She was the daughter of Philip Boutwell Davis and Dora Coon. The family lived in St. Louis where she went to school and in 1928 was graduated from Washington University with a major in psychology and Latin. The next year she received a masters' degree from the University of Chicago. The Phi Beta Kappa key was awarded to her at Washington University. Dr . Wilson McTeer and she were married on 3 July 1931 at her mother's home town of Charleston,Illinois. He was a professor of psychology at the College of the City of Detroit, which became Wayne State University. By avocation and later by profession, Frances was a genealogist. She was elected a Fellow of the American Society of Genealogists. Formerly she was editor of the Detroit Society for Genealogical Research Magazine. She wrote the genealogy of the Coon (Cohn) Family. Her collection consists of her materials on the Coon family and other related families.
