Biographical/Historical note

The League of Women Voters is an outgrowth of the National American Woman Suffrage Association. The purpose of the League has been to promote political responsibility through informed and active participation of citizens in government. Organized on three levels, national, state and local; the first regular meeting in Detroit of the new organization was held in December, 1919. Material reflecting the activities of this Detroit League, as well as the Michigan and National Leagues, are contained in this collection, with coverage concentrated upon the years 1950 to 1974.

Biographical/Historical note

Kathleen N. Straus (born 1923) was an active volunteer in many organizations. In addition to serving in the League of Women Voters, she was involved in various civil rights initiatives of the 1960s, such as the Mayor's Committee for Community Renewal.

Scope and Contents note

The history, minutes, and correspondence, of the League of Women Voters and papers reflecting the personal concerns of Kathleen Straus, its President (1961-63).
