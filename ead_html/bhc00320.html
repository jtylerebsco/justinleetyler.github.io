Biographical/Historical note

The F. Bruesser costume manufacturing company began in Detroit, Mich. on Macomb street in 1896. Sabrina and Frank Bruesser, sister and brother, ran the company, which eventually included their four brothers and father. Niece Helen (married name King) took over the company when her aunt and uncle died, moving the company to Ann Arbor, Mich. in 1985. The 100 year old company supplied thousands of costumes for theatrical productions throughout the United States, including plays, minstrel shows, high school musicals, and opera groups. The company was also, reportedly, the largest costume rental business in Michigan.

Scope and Contents note
Correspondence from all over the country came to the small company with requests ranging from cowboy costumes to Santa Claus wigs. One notable letter, included in the General Correspondence of 1948, is from Jack Benny's secretary, requesting a turban "for Eddie (Rochester) Anderson to be used at Palace Theatre".

Series III Costume Catalogs/Theater Programs
Scope and Contents note
Published catalogs from other costume companies, as well as newsletters from the National Costumers Association, are included here. Theater programs of various plays are also part of this series. They are arranged alphabetically. F. Bruesser and Co. received credit in a number of these programs for their role in costuming many of the productions.

Series IV Photographs
Scope and Contents note
Perhaps the most fascinating portion of the entire collection are the dramatic photographs of actors (and companies in costume. These date: h,ac approximately to 1901. Included with the Stage Costumes and Productions photos is a color snapshot of a company of "Taming of the Shrew", featuring costumer Helen King, the only person not in costume. Another interesting photograph is an unusual shot of a female magician levitating another woman. The magician has been identified as Adelaide Hermann, one of the few women who have performed magic professionally throughout history. Family photographs provide a wonderful history of the Bruesser family during the late 1800s to the early twentieth century.
