Biographical/Historical note

The Sorosis Literary and Art Club was founded on November 13, 1921 by founding members Mrs. William 0. Terrell, Mrs. Frank E. Dawson, Mrs. D.A. Stewart and Mrs. Alfred E. Thomas. These women, having moved to Detroit from the South shortly after World War I, organized this club where they could meet and share mutual interests in arts, crafts, literature, music and cultural events. The name Sorosis was selected because of its meaning-Greek for sisters. The club motto is "Utile Dulci" which means "The useful and the beautiful." Meetings today focus on stimulating discussions by guest speakers, art demonstrations, book reviews and travel and musical programs. The club maintains memberships in the Detroit Association of Women's Clubs, the Founder's Society of the Detroit Institute of Arts and the Friends of the Detroit Public Library.
