Abstract
Chiefly automotive-related drawings and photographs with some blueprints. Topics covered are: automobiles, 1900-1917; automotive design; Bi-Autogo; Da Vinci automobile; Scripps-Booth JB Rocket and Packet cyclecars; Stutz infringement suit; Victret phonograph.

Biographical/Historical note

Born May 31, 1888 in Detroit, Mich. to George and Ellen Booth, founders of Cranbrook Educational Community. Artist and automotive designer who also contributed to design features of the Cranbrook campus in Bloomfield Hills, Mich. His art work, chiefly pastels, is in the permanent collections of the Cranbrook Academy of Art Museum, and the Detroit Institute of Arts, among other locations. He died Sept. 13, 1954.

1888 James Scripps Booth born May 31 in Detroit.
1908 Began to sketch plans for the Bi-Autogo.
1910 Married Jean Alice McLaughlin on June 1.
1910 Wrote and illustrated literature for Hupp Motor Company.
1911-12 Travelled to Europe to study at the Ecole des Beaux Arts.
1911-12 Studied under Myron Barlow in Etaples, France.
1912 Took up residence in Highland Park, Michigan.
1912 Began to exhibit annually at the Scarab Club exhibition for Michigan artists at the Detroit Museum of Arts.
1912 Built Bi-Autogo.
1913 Bi-Autogo taken for test drive (in May).
1913 Scripps-Booth Cyclecar Company organized in Detroit.
1913 Designed JB Rocket and Packet cyclecars.
1913 First child, John McLaughlin Booth, born May 28.
1914 Scripps-Booth Company organized.
1915 Production of four cylinder Model C Scripps-Booth.
1916 Designed eight cylinder Model D Scripps-Booth in two body styles.
1916 Designed Model G roadster.
1916 Took the Hopkin Memorial second prize at the annual Scarab Club exhibition for Michigan artists at the Detroit Museum of Art for "The Dog Show".
1916 Daughter Margaret Ellen Booth born September 22.
1916 Resigned from Scripps-Booth Company.
1917 Completed commissions for Evening News Association and George Booth.
1917 Moved to Pasadena, California.
1917 Listed in Who's Who in American Art.
1917 Daughter Ann Louise Booth born December 20.
1919 Painted the Still Room ceiling in Cranbrook House.
1920 Spring Morning took third prize at the annual Scarab Club exhibition for Michigan artists at the DIA.
1920 Relocated to new residence in California.
1921 Began designing Da Vinci automobile.
1923 Moved to Tower Cottage, Cranbrook.
1923 Worked with George Booth to help design North Terrace stairway and other features at Cranbrook.
1923 Designed Da Vinci Pup cyclecar.
1925 Lawsuit filed against Stutz Company for infringing Da Vinci patents.
1930 Moved to Indian Village, Detroit.
1935 Awarded Judgement in Stutz suit.
1942 Published General Handbook, Motor Mechanics Simplified: Understand Your Car.
1943 Married Ellen Catherine Norlen on February 20.
1949-51 Edited Cyril Player's manuscript The Life of George Gough Booth.
1951 Moved to Sun House in New Canaan, Connecticut.
1954 Published Adventures in Analysis, A Searching Biblical Commentary.
1954 Died at the age of 66 on September 13.
