Biographical/Historical note

Born in the "Thumb" area of Michigan on Feb. 21, 1873, the son of Charles E. and Elizabeth (McDonald) Thompson. Graduated from the University of Michigan in 1896, LL.B. Practiced law in Sad Axe, Mich. and active in the Republican Party, the Presbyterian Church, and the Knights Templar. Delegate to the Michigan Constitutional Convention (1907-1908). Active in local business, director of the State Savings Bank and Consolidated Telephone. Married Ida Elizabeth Proctor, Jan. 10, 1912. (from Moore. History of Michigan. v. 4. p. 1320.) (blue index cards)
