Biographical/Historical note

Born in New York, N.Y., 1801; moved to Philadelphia area, 1822; founder of Centerville, Mich. ; Died in Paducah, Kentucky, 1854.,The son of William and Susan Elliott Langley, Thomas W. Langley was born in New York in 1801. He and Margaret Stigman married on Mar. 22, 1822 in Philadelphia, Penn., where he had a dry goods store. In 1832 he bought a site of land in Centerville, Mich. and laid out the first building for the seat of St. Joseph County. Langley had a hotel, blacksmith shop, store, saw and gristmills. He taught the first school in Centerville and was postmaster from 1833-1840. Circa 1851 he closed out his interests in St. Joseph County and formed a mercantile agency in Philadelphia, traveling through the South for several jobbing houses. Thomas Langley died in Paducah, Kentucky in 1854 as a result of injuries from a fall.

Scope and Contents note

This collection consists chiefly of correspondence of members of the Langley family in Michigan and Pennsylvania, relating largely to pioneer and business conditions, together with earlier letters from seamen friends.
