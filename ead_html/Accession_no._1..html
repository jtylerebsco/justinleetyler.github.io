Abstract
Important subjects covered are: American technical mission to India, 1942; armored vehicles, 1930-1942; civilian and military vehicles, 1930-1942; Indian industrial conversion; the Marmon-Herrington Co.; and advertisements and photographs of armored vehicles. Notable correspondents include Gen. George S. Patton and Gen. Claire L. Chennault.

Biographical/Historical note

Arthur W. Herrington was born in Coddenham, England on March. 30, 1891. He immigrated with his parents to Madison, New Jersey then he was five years old. After attending public schools in Madison, he received his advanced technical education at the Stevens Institute of Technology in Hoboken, New Jersey, He was first employed by the Harley-Davidson Motor Company in Milwaukee, Wisconsin as a Assistant Chief Engineer. During World War I, he served in France with the Second Divisional Supply Train/Motor Transport Corps. Upon return to civilian life, he worked as a Consulting Engineer to the American military. During the 1920's and throughout his life he received various American and for -eign patents. In 1931, he formed along with auto pioneer Walter C. Marmon the Marmon-Herrington Company, Inc producers of various all-wheel-drive civilian and military vehicles. Mr. Herrington was an active observer of the worldwide military buildup of war material. In 1942 he was elected president of the Society of Automotive Engineers. President Franklin Roosevelt appointed him in March 1942 to the "American Technical Mission To India" to aid India in its industrial conversion. After World War Its Mrs Herrington continued to run the Marmon-Herrington Company, Inc, until his retirement in 1960. He also served as Director of the Society of American Military Engineers and on the Competition Panel for the United States Auto Clubs He was also on the Board of the American Automobile Association for racing contests. Mr. Herrington died on September 69 1970. The papers of Mr. Herrington reflect his wok within the M arion-Herrington Company particularly during its initial years in the 1930's and, his work for the American government as a military advisor before and during World War II.
