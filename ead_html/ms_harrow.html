Biographical/Historical note

The Harrow family is one of the oldest families in St. Clair County, Michigan. The first to come to Michigan was Alexander Harrow. Born in Aberdeenshire, Scotland in 1755, he went to Canada at the age of twenty, leaving behind three children and his young wife's grave. Harrow became a lieutenant in the British Navy for the defense of Quebec in 1775 and remained in command on the Great Lakes. In 1782 Harrow was transferred to the Gage, at the time the largest vessel on the Upper Lakes, and in 1785 he was put in charge of the Rebecca, a new schooner even larger than the Gage. During all the time of his service on the Lakes he made daily entries in his logbooks. Harrow settled at Detroit in 1794, though he was still a British officer. He and his second wife Catherine had five children: John George, James, Catherine (wife of James Dunlap) and Mary (wife of Stephen Grummond Sr.). Alexander Harrow lived on his Algonac farm until his death in 1811.

Biographical/Historical note

James P. Harrow was born in St. Clair County on January 23, 1839, the son of George and Lucile Peer Harrow. He married Marcia J. Lyons on March 20, 1867 and was a builder and master of boats on the Great Lakes. The tugboat "Young America" was build by James Harrow and operated in partnership with his brother, William G. Record.
