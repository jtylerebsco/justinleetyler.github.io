Biographical/Historical note

Stefan (Stef) Florescu was born in Chicago on December 26, 1926 and was raised in Davenport, Iowa. After service in the U.S. Army, he completed his education in Davenport where he received a B.A. degree in sociology from St. Ambrose College in 1951. At St. Ambrose he earned four varsity letters in intercollegiate track, and was elected "Track Athlete of the Year" in his senior year. A diving accident in 1952 resulted in Mr. Florescu becoming a quadriplegic, but he remained physically active in worldwide wheelchair competitions. In the 1964 Tokyo Wheelchair Olympics he won a gold medal in Table Tennis and silver and bronze medals in Swimming. He was a founder of numerous organizations and publications, including the Annual Michigan Wheelchair Games, Michigan Paralyzed Veterans of American, Detroit's Architectural Barriers Committee and editor-publisher of "The Wheelchair Competitor", the first magazine devoted exclusively to reporting and promoting wheelchair athletics.
