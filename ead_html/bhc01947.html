Title: Cornelius J. O'Flynn papers,
Dates: 1785-1906.
Quantity: 1.8 linear ft. (3 boxes, 1 volume)
Abstract: This collection contains Mr. O'Flynn's legal papers, arranged alphabetically by client. It also includes ajournal kept while studying law in Detroit, 1859-1861, containing both a skeleton diary and legal memoranda and data. The legal papers include deeds, indentures, receipts, correspondence on pending cases, court records of testimony taken, etc. The documents of earlier dates include such things as marriage contracts, property agreements and other documents which played a role in legal actions of a later generation.
Repository: Detroit Public Library. Burton Historical Collection.

Biographical/Historical note

Detroit city attorney, 1842, and probate judge.

Biographical/Historical note

Born in Tralee, Ireland in 1810, Cornelius O'Flynn came to the United States about 1828. He was admitted t the bar at Detroit in 1834 and became City Attorney in 1842. Two years later he was appointed Judge of Probate Court, on which he remained until 1852. He also served as Postmaster of Detroit for some time, and was a representative from Wayne County, 1857-1858. Cornelius O'Flynn died in Detroit in 1869.
