General Physical Description note (27 boxes, 26 volumes)
Abstract The papers of the St. Andrew's Society reflect the organization's charitable and cultural activities, as well as the activities of the Ladies Auxiliary and the Highlanders. In addition to these three organizations, this collection also contains the papers of the Clan Campbell, Chapter #206, and the Lady Campbell, Chapter #78, for the years 1920 through 1961, and the papers of the Royal Neighbors of America, for the years 1921 through 1935.
Repository: Detroit Public Library. Burton Historical Collection.

Biographical/Historical note

The St. Andrew's Society of Detroit was organized in 1849 by William Barclay and a number of Scottish residents of the city. The three main objectives of the Society are: to relieve Scots who are in need, both overseas and in the United States; to foster and encourage a love of Scotland, its history, literature, music and national games; and to promote friendly and social relations among its members and among all persons of Scottish ancestry. In addition to providing assistance to Scots in Detroit and in Scotland, the Society participated in a program of raising aid for British soldiers wounded or imprisoned during World War II and for the widows and dependents of British soldiers killed in action. Among the annual social events sponsored by the Society are the Robert Burns Concert and Ball in February, the Highland Picnic and Games in August, and a St. Andrew's Day Banquet in November. The St. Andrew's Society is open to all Scotsmen and their descendants. The elected officers of the Society are the president, two vice presidents, the financial secretary and a librarian. The appointed officers include a chaplain, a bard, an organist, and others. The general business meetings of the Society are held monthly and are open to all members.The Society is governed by an elected seven-member Board of Trustees which has general control of financial affairs. The Board receives all money turned over by the treasurer and invests it in the name of the Society. It also manages the Society's property and the St. Andrew's Hall. Throughout the years, this Hall has been the meeting place for many organizations in the city. The hall on E. Congress St. was closed in 1994. The Ladies Auxiliary of the St. Andrew's Society was formed in 1910. The Auxiliary is open to mothers, wives, sisters and daughters of members of the Society in good standing. The St. Andrew's Highlanders are the Pipe and Drum Corps of the Society. All members of the Society in good standing are eligible to join the Highlanders.

General Note
Correspondence, clippings, financial reports, newsletters, programs and other material from office files of the St. Andrew's Society. Series II:
General Note
Correspondence, minute books, financial recordS, cemetery deeds, hall leases, insurance policies, stock certificates and other items created by the Board of Trustees of the Society. The Trustees controlled the finances of the Society, receiving money from the treasurer and investing it in the name of the Society. They also controlled and supervised the cemetery plots bought by the Society for the benefit of Scottish citizens of Detroit. However, most of this series concerns the management of the St. Andrew's Hall at 431 East Congress. The Trustees raised the money to build the hall through the sale of stock certificates, supervised its improvement and maintaintence, and rented the Hall to various organizations in the city. The papers are arranged by subject or type of material.

Series III: Minutes, 1905 - 1975
General Note
This series contains minute books from the regular business meetings of the St. Andrew's Society covering the years 1905-1920 (when the Society held bi-monthly meetings) and 1932-1954 and 1963-1969 (when the society held monthly meetings). Also included in these books are the minutes of special meetings held at various times over the years. This series also contains material removed from these minute books, such as programs, financial reports, clippings, etc. The materials removed from the books are arranged in chronological order. See Volumes at the end of this Finding Aid for Society Minute Books.

Series IV: Membership. 1849 - 1991
General Note
Applications, lists, index card files, committee reports, dues books, resignations, sympathy cards and other items documenting the membership of the Society throughout its years in Detroit. In addition to members' names, these papers also include the brief family history and employment information that members supplied in order to join the Society. Lists of officers, resolutions of regret on the death of Society members, and thank you cards from the families of deceased members are also included in this series. The papers are arranged alphabetically. See Volumes at the end of this Finding Aid for Membership Dues Books and Roll Books. See Boxes 25-27 for Index Card Files.
