Biographical/Historical note

Jamison Handy--better known as "Jam"--was born in Philadelphia in 1886. The youngest of seven children, he was the son of Sarah Matthews and Major Moses P. Handy. His father was a newspaper editor for various publications including the Chicago Times-Herald, and his mother was also a writer, with her fiction and verse published in numerous magazines, among them Godey's Book. As a boy, young Handy became a strong swimmer, winning prizes at the local YMCA. He was so successful, in fact, that in 1904 he became a member of the U.S. Olympic Swim Team. Twenty years later he would again participate in the Olympic games, winning him a place in "Ripley's Believe It or Not". After attending the University of Michigan, Handy followed in his father's footsteps in becoming a newspaperman. He worked on the Chicago Tribune before forming an educational advertising agency with Herbert Kaufman. In 1916, Handy joined with James Keeley to form the Keeley-Handy Syndicate, a motion picture production company, which eventually included the Bray Studios of New York. Newspapers Film Corporation was the successor to the Keeley-Handy Syndicate, with Jam Handy acting as general manager. Jam Handy married Helen Hoag Rogers and had five children. Mrs. Handy died in 1973. Jam Handy died in 1983, having lived a long, active life. He swan regularly well into his nineties. This collection includes personal and company correspondence, photographs, memorabilia and newspaper clippings of this noteworthy man.

Series I: Jamison Handy 

Summary and contents note

Born in 1886, Jam Handy lived to the age of ninety-seven, dying in Detroit in 1983. Throughout his life he maintained close contact with his family, first his parents, then his brothers and sisters, and later his wife, children, grandchilren, nieces and nephews. He also had many friends, some of whom were quite prominent in their various fields. Among the Personal Correspondence are letters from Admiral Arthur W. Radford (Box 4:3), Vice-Admiral Calvin Durgin (Box 3:1) and an autographed picture of General Jimmy Doolittle (Box 5:2). Other items of interest in this series include early report cards and children's school reports in Box 5:1, and poems written by Jam Handy (Box 5:4). The oldest dated item in the collection is a letter from young Jamison to his father, written in 1894 (Box 2:4).

Series II: Jam Handy Organization 

Summary and contents note

Formed in 1928, the Jam Handy Organization enjoyed many awards, as did its creator. The highlight was probably "Jam Handy Day", in 1961. Detroit Mayor Louis Miriani presented Jam Handy with a special award in honor of the organization's 50th anniversary (Box 7:5). Publicity and photographs of numerous celebrities are also included here, including a letter from Michigan Governor William Milliken in Box 8:1.

Series III: Keeley-Handy Syndicate 

Summary and contents note

Before Jam Handy went out on his own, he was in a partnership with newspaperman James Keeley. This company was a pioneer in the motion picture industry, using film as a means of education and advertising, as well as entertainment. Correspondence dates from 1916 in this series.

Series V: Swimming 

Summary and contents note

As a champion swimmer, Jam Handy participated in the 1904 and 1924 Olympic games. A photograph with President Gerald Ford is included in this series, in Box 14:10. Some of the earliest items in the collection are also part of this series, with newspaper articles dating back to 1905 (see also XLMS) and a program for a Special Match Race in 1906 (Box 13:5).
