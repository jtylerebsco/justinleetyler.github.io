Biographical/Historical note

Benjamin Laikin was born in 1896 in a small town in Russia where he received a traditional Jewish education. He came to America in 1914, landing at Baltimore, Maryland. A few months later he went to Philadelphia, and in January, 1915, he was in Gardner, Massachusetts. There he worked for almost four years in a junkshop, followed by a short stint in a coal mine in Pennsylvania. He returned to Baltimore where he married Chana (Anna) Leah Golomb in 1922. They became the parents of four daughters. After being in business for himself for awhile, Laikin worked as a traveling agent for a rag firm, which took him all over the United States and Canada, as well as Europe and Japan. In 1929 he came to Detroit. Laikin created a successful wiping cloth factory, the Consumers Paper Company. He obtained contracts during World War II with the Federal Government for his products. In 1970, Laikin was named Honorary Director of the National Association of Wiping Cloth Manufacturers.
