Biographical/Historical note

Fred R. Schmalzriedt was a Detroit lawyer, Spanish War veteran and former city of Detroit official. A 1901 graduate of the Detroit College of Law, Mr. Schmalzriedt worked as a lawyer in the Detroit City Clerk's Office. In 1909 and 1912 he was named to edit Detroit's compiled ordinances. As a lawyer, Mr. Schmalzriedt took part in more than one hundred cases involving election disputes, and in 1930 was attorney for the committee which brought about the recalI of Detroit Mayor Charles E. Bowles. Schmalzriedt was a veteran of the Spanish-American War, and in 1908 was elected commander of the Michigan Department of United Spanish War Veterans. He was also president of the Detroit Philatelic Society and belonged to various other national and local historical and collectors groups. He and his wife Mildred had three children. Fred R. Schmalzriedt died in Detroit in October 1960.
