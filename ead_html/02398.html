Providence, R.I., 17 Sept. 1756. Deed. 

Scope and Contents note

David Thayer, yeoman & his wife Rebekah Thayer to Abraham Whipple, mariner fora In old tenor money, conveying a lot in Providence on Back street adjoining land of the heirs of William Brown and of David Thayer and Joseph Oven. Witnesses Joseph Rhodes & George Taylor, justice of the peace. Recorded in book 15 on page 150 by Nich. Tillinghast, town clerk.

Providence, R.I., 27 Nov., 1756. Deed 

Scope and Contents note

Abraham Whipple, mariner to Noah Whipple, distiller, for £700 old tenor bills of the colony, a lot on Back street in Providence adjoining land of Joseph Owen, and the heirs of William Brown deceased. Witnesses Robert Brown and Thomas Birkett and acknowledge before George Taylor justice of the peace. Recorded in book 16 on page 50 by James Angell, town clerk.

Scope and Contents note

Amasa Killem of Providence in the Colony of Rhode Island, yeoman, to Abraham Whipple, yeoman, for L200 old tenor money of the Colony aforesaid, for two lots in Providence, on Back street, adjoining lands of William Brown, David Thayer & Joseph Owen. Witnesses Nich. Brown and John Brown.

Providence, R.I., Jany 16, 1769. Mortgage. 

Scope and Contents note

Abraham Whipple, mariner, and Sarah his wife to Simeon Potter, of Bristol, for five hundred Spanish milled dollars, of land in Providence. Witnessed by Simeon Munro and Simeon Potter Cockrum. Acknowledged before Simeon Munro, justice of the peace. Recorded: Providence in book 18 on page 309, by Dan Marsh, Dep. Clk. Released 23 April 1770 and release witnessed by Archibald Munro and Sarah Munro.

Providence, R.I., July 8, 1773. Indentures. 

Scope and Contents note

Henry Northrup, son of George Northrup late of North Kingston in King's County, deceased with the consent of his mother Francis Northrup binds himself to Abraham Whipple, mariner, as an apprentice to serve 6 years 9 months and 6 days, to be instructed in the seamans and mariners art, at the end of this time the apprentice is to have a new beaver hat and new suit of clothes, together with common wearing apparel and also a set of instruments and books suitable for a mariner.

Return to Table of Contents »

March 1, 1779. Bill of Sale. 

Scope and Contents note

Thomas Simpson, lieut. in the American Navy and commander of the continental ship of War Ranger, for £10000. sells & conveys to Abraham Whipple, Captain in the American Navy and commander of the continental ship of War Providence one full seaman's share that may arise from all & singular the prizasthat shall or may be taken or seized during the present cruize of the ship of War Ranger, from the day of her now departure until arrived again in the thirteen United States of America. Witnesses Samuel Tucker and Thos Jonson.

Cranston, R.I., 1 Oct. 1779. Deed. 

Scope and Contents note

Henerery Randall, miller, to Abraham Whipple of Providence for £391 a strip of land on the easterly side of the highway leading from the house of Samuel Tomkins to a place called Monkey Town. (Location of land not given) Witnesses Benoni Pearce and Andrew Harris. Recorded in Cranston in book 3 page 162, by Nehemiah Knight. Town elk. Grantor signs Henry Randall.

Providence, R.I. Dec. 8, 1779. Lease. 

Scope and Contents note

Capt. Abraham Whipple has purchased a farm of William Hopkins of Voluntown, Connecticut where said William now dwells. Said William may retain possession of thefarm until 25 March 1780. Signed by Sarah Whipple and William Hopkins but not by Abraham Whipple. Witness Jno. Mumford.

Providence, R.I., April 1, 1783. Mortgage. 

Scope and Contents note

Abraham Whipple of Cranston, R.I. to John Brown, merchant of Providence, conveying "my mansion house & small lot" in Cranston, bound by lands of Henry Randall. Also other lands in Cranston near Hawkins Hole, for £430 9s. Witnesses George Warner and John Foster Justice of the Peace. Recorded by Nehemiah Knight, T. Clerk.

Providence, R.I. 21 Sept. 1787. Appointment. 

Scope and Contents note

The Subscribers, Associates in the Ohio Company, do hereby constitute and appoint Ebenezer Sproat, Esq. Agent for our Respective Shares agreeable to the original Articles of Association by us severally subscribed. In Witness whereof we have hereunto severally set our Hands & Seals at Providence In the State of Rhode Island, September 21st, 1787. Appended are the signatures of Abraham & Mary Whipple & 27 others.

Cumberland, Oct. 1, 1787, Receipt. 

Scope and Contents note

Ebz. Sproat, Agent, acknowledges that has received of Colonel Coggeshall Olney $2000 Continental Specie certificates which constitutes him the proprietor of two shares of the funds of the Ohio Company. John S. Dexter attaches an acknowledgement of $20 in silver "in full for the specie deposit on the above mentioned two shares" dated May 28, 1788.
