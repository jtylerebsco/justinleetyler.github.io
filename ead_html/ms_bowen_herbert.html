Abstract: This collection contains correspondence and other papers, chiefly from 1835 to 1900, and early bank notes. Many of the letters pertain to lecture and dramatic performances in Detroit. Correspondents include John Judson Bagley, James Valentine Campbell, Thomas M. Cooley, Henry Howland Crapo and Samuel T. Douglass.
Language: In English.
Repository: Detroit Public Library. Burton Historical Collection.

Biographical/Historical note

Born in East Aurora, New York on January 20, 1847, Herbert Bowen began the study of law with Benjamin F. Graves in Battle Creek at the age of sixteen. He settled in Detroit and practiced law with Judge Samuel T. Douglass. Bowen was a member of the Detroit Library Commission from 1885-1897 and 1905-1910. The Bowen Branch Library is named for him in honor of the large contributions he made to the Library. He married Lydien Graves in 1876 and they had five children. Herbert Bowne died in Detroit on December 17, 1921.
