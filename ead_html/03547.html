Abstract
Transcripts and tape recorded interviews of the 41 African Americans in health care or related fields, who participated in this project. Interviews give biographical details and examine issues of racial discrimination, segregated health care and desegregation efforts in Detroit area hospitals, the impact of labor and migration patterns on medical care for African Americans, and social conditions. Includes printed copies digital photographs.

Scope and Contents note

The following interviews are not included in the subject index. Each has an oral interview guide and a blue binder containing a biography and a transcript of the interview. Included with some interviews are 1 resumes or articles or clippings or publications or manuscripts of correspondence or photographs. The audio tapes for each interviewee are stored in a smaller container placed in the box.
