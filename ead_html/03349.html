Biographical/Historical note

Raymond Joseph Meurer was born in Detroit in 1904. He joined WWJ radio in 1922 as a broadcaster. In 1931 he graduated from the Detroit College of Law. He became counsel for the American Federation of Musicians, the United Detroit Theaters, the Michigan Radio Network, and radio station WXYZ. In the 1930's he became an officer, director and general counsel of the Lone Ranger Inc. as part of the firm of Treadle, Campbell, Meurer Inc. Mr. Meurer retired in 1954 but 2 years later he resumed work as advertising and sales promotion manager of Michigan Consolidated Gas Co. He retired from the Gas Company in 1968. Under Mr. Meurer Trendle, Campbell, Meurer Inc. developed the Lone Ranger, The Green Hornet, Sergeant Preston of the Yukon, and the Silver Eagle. Mr. Meurer died October 15, 1974 (from Detroit News - October 18, 1974)
