Abstract: Subjects covered include early automobile production at Standard Wheel and Overland Co., cost analysis at General Motors in 1912, scientific commercial research and projected cyclcecar manufacture. Important correspondents include Banus Neal, Scott Prime, C.L. Wood and C.G. Fisher. Includes photographs of Cox and his family, of the Wilcox automobile, the Overland and Wilcox factories and of Commercial Engineering Labs.
Repository: Detroit Public Library. National Automotive History Collection.

Biographical/Historical note

Automotive engineer, designed and built the first Overland, president of Overland Car Co., director of research at General Motors, president of Commercial Engineering Laboratories and of Barlett Avenue Associates, among other positions.

June 1902 Standard Wheel Co., 639 Ohio St., Terre Haute, In.
1903 Superintendant, Standard Wheel Co.
1904-5 Manager, Standard Wheel Co., Indpls, In.
1906-7 President, Overland Car Co., Indpls, In.
1908-9 Chief Engineer and Assistant Manager, Interstate Automobile Co., Muncie, In.
1910-11 Factory Manager, H. E. Wilcox Motor Car Co., Minneapolie, Mn.
1912 Director of Research, General Motors Co., Detroit, Ni.
1913 General Manager, Commercial Engineering Laboratories, Detroit, Ni.
1915 Director, Commercial Engineering Laboratories
1943 President, Commercial Research Laboratories, Inc., Detroit, Mi.
1955 President, Barlett Avenue Associates, Inc. Detroit, Mi.
