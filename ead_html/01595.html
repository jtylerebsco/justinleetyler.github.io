Abstract
In 1964, the Board of American Missions of the Lutheran Church of America, in conjunction with the Michigan Synod, decided to begin a mission in Detroit at the East Vernor-St. Jean area. By May 1965, Pastor George Housewright was holding services at Tendler School. In 1967, Pastor Dennis Holmberg became the Assistant, and later Pastor and Mission Developer of the Lutheran Church of the Cross. A renovation began in 1968 that increased the use of space in the building for more church activities. In October 1972, the Charter was opened and by 1973 the Church of the Cross was formally received as a congregation of the Michigan Synod of the Lutheran Church in America.
