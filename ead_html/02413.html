Biographical/Historical note

Polish American physician and mayor, of Hamtramck, Mich.,Dr. Skrzycki was born in Buffalo, N.Y. on July 29, 1893. He graduated from St. John's College, Philadelphia, on June 25, 1912, in Polish philology. His M. D. degree he received from Temple University, in 1917, and had membership in H. Brooker Mills Pediatric Society and Applegate Obstetrical Society. He interned at St. Mary's Hospital, in Detroit, in 1918, and was licensed to practice medicine on March 20, 1919. In 1937, he was the only Michigan surgeon appointed Fellow of the American College of Surgeons (27 members). He became a registered pharmacist in 1942. During 35 years of practice in Hamtramck, he delivered 11,000 babies. He was elected Mayor of Hamtramck five times from 1942-1952, and was noted for removing corruption from local politics, holding fair election campaigns, and establishing sound fiscal government. He was a gifted violinist and performed professionally in his youth. He established the Hamtramck chapter of the Society for the Preservation and Encouragement of Barber Shop Quartet Singing in America. Also active in numerous Polish and professional organizations. He died of throat cancer on August 4, 1954. (blue index cards)

Scope and Contents note

Scrapbooks relating chiefly to Skrzycki's activities as mayor of Hamtramck, Mich.; together with awards, citations, diplomas, memberships, condolences, photos, and clippings, relating to his medical career, politics, and music, including Society for the Preservation and Encouragement of Barber Shop Quartet Singing in America, Hamtramck, Mich., Chapter.
