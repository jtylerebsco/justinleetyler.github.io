Biographical/Historical note

A pianist, composer, choral director, conductor and music pedagogue, he was born in Poznan, Poland on Sept, 14, 1898 and emigrated to the U.S. in 1902. He studied piano with James McLaughlin and Godowski. At 18, he accompanied many great singers on the piano in Troy, N.Y. He served in the US Army during WWI. In 1923, he came to station WWJ in Detroit as an entertainer. Next, for 7 years, he was musical director at Alliance College in Cambridge, PA. In 1940, he became choral director in Iutian Singing Society until 1959, presenting new Polish works, his own compositions, operas, conducting the DSO or in piano recitals. He was honored many times with banquets and musical tributes. At 62 he was honored by the Polish Govt. in Miriani with the "key" to Detroit. He died on August 3, 1963.
