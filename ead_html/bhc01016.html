Biographical/Historical note

Corporation counsel for the City of Detroit.Born in London, Ontario in 1895, Nathaniel H. Goldstick came to Detroit with his parents in 1901. He graduated from the University of Michigan in 1915. Mr. Goldstick served in World War I, coming out as a second lieutenant in the Field Artillery. He married Ella Goldstein in 1918 and they had four children. After graduating from the U-M Law School he was assigned in 1926 as counsel for the Detroit Police Department. His proudest moment came in 1929 when he was up against the noted attorney Clarence Darrow in a censorship case - and won. In 1958 he became corporation counsel for the City of Detroit. Active in many organizations, Mr. Goldstick served on the board of Temple Israel; was president of the Old Newsboys' Goodfellow Fund and Jewish Child Placement Bureau; and was a member of the Knights of Pythias and the Masons. He taught a law class for police officers at Wayne State University for over twenty years. In 1962 he retired from the City of Detroit and opened his own law firm in the Penobscot Building. Nathaniel H. Goldstick died in Detroit in 1981 at the age of 87.
