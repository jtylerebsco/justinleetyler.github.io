Title: Dutilh Family papers,
Dates: 1755-1875.
Quantity: 32 linear ft. (64 boxes, 2 large manuscript, 2 microfilm reels)
Abstract: The effect on trade conditions of the French and American revolutions, difficulties with Great Britain culminating in the Embargo Act, emigrants selling their services for a term of years in return for passage to America, and plans for construction of slave dwellings are some of the subjects of interest.
Language: In English, French, Spanish, German, and Dutch.
Repository: Detroit Public Library. Burton Historical Collection.

Biographical/Historical note

Etienne Dutilh established the firm of Etienne Dutilh & Company on Oct. 19, 1789. Etienne Dutilh was in Rotterdam in 1771; in London in 1777; and in Philadelphia in 1789, where on October 19 he established the firm of Etienne Dutilh and Company. In 1755 the members of the family were in France and Holland, and soon afterward in England, Smyrna, and the West Indies.
