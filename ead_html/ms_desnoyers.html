Biographical/Historical note

Peter J. Desnoyers was born in Paris, France on Aug 1, 1772. He came to America in 1790, first settling in Gallipolis, Ohio, and moving to Detroit six years later. He was a silversmith by trade, and was commissioned by Col. Hamtramck as an armorer until 1803. At the time Desnoyers joined in business with J.B. Piquette, but they lost all in the Detroit fire of 1805. He later built a house and opened a general store. Desnoyers was one of the first Trustees of the University of Michigan. He also served as director of the Bank of Detroit, and became the bank's president in 1824. He died on June 3, 1846.
