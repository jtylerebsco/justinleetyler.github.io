Biographical/Historical note

William Robert Wilson was born October 8, 1884 in Chicago, the oldest child of James Henry and Hannah Krause Wilson. After graduating in 1906 from Chicago's Armour Institute of Technology with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Mechanical Engineering, Mr. Wilson obtained a job with Corn Products Refining Company, where he stayed less than a year. He was later employed by the Morgan Construction Company, a manufacturer of furnaces, kilns and gas producers, from 1907-1910. A photo album of the company's Gary, Indiana plant is included in this collection. Mr. Wilson went on to work for a number of various organizations, including several automotive companies and financial institutions, such as Studebaker, Dodge Brothers, Irving National Bank, Maxwell Motors, Reo Motor Car Company and Guardian Trust Company. He left his position as assistant to the Dodge Brothers in 1918 to become vice-president of Irving National Bank in New York City. In 1921 he was president of the Maxwell Motor Corporation and was named president of the Guardian Trust Company of Detroit four years later. The 1930's saw him as vice-president and general manager of Reo Motor Car Company, Lansing; head of Allied Motors; and president and general manager of Copeland Products, Inc. in Mt. Clemens. He also served as general chairman for the Greater Detroit Committee and the Mayor's Port Commission, as well as a member of the Board of Directors of the Detroit YMCA. In 1909 W.R. Wilson married Gertrude Gray of Minnesota, daughter of Henry Alexander and Annie Gray. They had seven children. Gertrude Wilson died November 7, 1952. Two years later, Mr. Wilson married Catherine Clapp, a secretary with the Gray-Wilson Company. William Robert Wilson died on June 6, 1958 at his home in Grosse Pointe.

Summary

These, in fact, constitute the oldest items in the collection, as they were owned by Hannah Krause (Wilson) in 1865, when she was eight years old. Family papers include birth certificates of the Wilson family members.

Sub-series A: Automotive Industry
Summary
From Studebaker in 1911 to the Dodge Brothers one year later, from Maxwell-Chalmers in 1921, Murray Corporation of America in 1926, Reo Motor Car Co., 1927 and Continental Motors in 1928, W.R. Wilson covered a wide number of early automobile manufacturers. On the request of his friend Walter P. Chrysler, Mr. Wilson Became head of the reorganization of Maxwell Motor Corp. in Detroit in 1921. In 1930 he became general manager of Reo Motor Car Company. He was also made president and chairman of the board of Murray Corp.
