Biographical/Historical note

Born in Warsaw, Poland on June 16, 1902. She emigrated with her family to the U.S.), first settling in Cleveland, OH and in 1916, in Detroit. She attended Northeastern High School, later City Colleges and Detroit Teachers College, where she and her sister, Jeannette Pawlowska, received their teacher's certificates. After 20 years, she returned to obtain her BA and MA in education from Wayne State University. Her masters thesis on Polish folksongs (in 1943), was published by Wayne State U. Press in 1961 and again in 1983. In the summer of 1961, MERRILY WE SING received the Chicago Folklore Prize and was also awarded by the 6th Midwestern Books Competition. After her MA, she taught English at Pershing High School for another 23 years. Miss Pawlowska was a founding member of the Friends of Polish Art and acted as its secretary. In 1945, she helped organize the first Polish art exhibition at the Detroit Institute of Art, and prepared its exhibition catalog. (See Box 1 of the Friends of Polish Art Papers.) After a long and active cultural life, Miss Pawlowska died on April 6, 1993.
