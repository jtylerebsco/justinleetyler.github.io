Biographical/Historical note

Clarence A. Reid was born in Circleville, Ohio in 1892, the son of George W. and Samantha Strawser Reid. After attending the University of Michigan Law School, he was admitted to the Michigan State Bar in 1921. Following his admission to the bar, Mr. Reid practiced law in the city of Detroit. Clarence Reid married Grace M. Mapes on March 25, 1920, and they had three children. They made their home in Detroit. He was elected to the Michigan State Senate in 1941, where he remained until becoming Lieutenant Governor of Michigan in 1953.
