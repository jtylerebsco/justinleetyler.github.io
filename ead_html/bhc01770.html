Biographical/Historical note

John Meyer was born in Bremen, Germany on December 4, 1855, where, before he was fourteen years of age, he developed an interest in astronomy. On November 30, 1872 he sailed for America, Arriving in New York with only fifty cents in his pocket. Within a few years he came to Detroit, where he was an apprentice to Theodore Moser, pharmacist. Meyer married Mr. Moser's daughter Bertha on April 14, 1880. Meyer became a very skillful analytical chemist and did more for the Mount Clemens mineral bath industry than any other single person. From his arrival in Mt. Clemens in 1880 until his death in 1844, Meyer was deeply interested in the curative effects of Mt. Clemens' waters. When John Meyer died in Algonac, Michigan on September 1, 1944, he was Michigan's oldest chemist.
