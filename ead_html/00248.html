Biographical/Historical note

Helen Carolyn Bower was born in Michigan on January 7, 1896, to D. Herbert and Amelia Bower. She came from a newspaper family: her father owned and edited the Buchanan (Mich.) Record, and her grandfather, Daniel Bower, had owned a newspaper in Williamsport, Penn. before the Civil War. By 1910, the family was living in Detroit, Mich. After graduating from high school Bower got a job at the Detroit Free Press in 1914, covering women's clubs. She left the paper to attend the University of Michigan, but returned in 1916 to become Assistant Society Editor. Her father was killed in that same year in an automobile accident. Though Helen had announced her engagement in 1918 to U.S. Army Lieutenant Daniel W. Kent, son of Mr. and Mrs. Clarence D. Kent of Buchanan, they were never married. The 1920 Federal Census shows Daniel Kent, age 24, living in Buchanan with his parents, working as an office clerk. Helen was active in a number of organizations and served on the board of Women's Hospital, Women's City Club, Friends of the Detroit Public Library, and two sororities. She worked for the National War Work Council Overseas Service during World War I, and spent time in 1922 Japan and other Far East countries.

Sub-series B: Diaries and Scrapbooks 

Scope and Contents note

Helen Bower kept two types of diaries. One was a day-book account, which are contained in small hooks in Boxes 7 and 8. The others were kept in loose-leaf binders which also served as scrapbooks. Most of the material in these larger diaries were left loose, between pages. These items have been removed and are located in Boxes 8-15, with the appropriate headings. The diaries themselves are listed under "Volumes". Among the material from the diaries are correspondence, newspaper clippings, photographs and theater programs. Notable autographs include those of Mary Pickford (Box 9:9); Edgar A. Guest (Box 12:5; 13:1; 15:3), photos of Eleanor Roosevelt (11:4) and Rosalind Russell (15:6).

Scope and Contents note

Sub-series D: Organizations 

Scope and Contents note

Helen was a member of many organizations, and served on the board of directors for several of them, including Friends of the Detroit Public Library, Women's City Club, Kappa Kappa Gamma and Theta Sigma Phi sororities. She was also active in the Lutheran church and served as editor for many organizational publications.

Series II: Movies and Entertainment 

Scope and Contents note

As movie critic for the Detroit Free Press for fifteen years, Helen Bower accumulated a vast amount of material on the topic, from movie magazines to press kits, theater programs to movie scripts. Helen even made an appearance in a major film, "Teacher's Pet", starring Clark Gable. Correspondence (Box 21:6) includes autographs of Jerry Lewis, Tom Patterson and Agnes Moorehead.
