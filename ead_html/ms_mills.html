Biographical/Historical note

Born in Greenville, Michigan on August 11, 1894, Joseph Mills came to Detroit in 1912. He attended Cass High School in Detroit, and New York University. Rejected by the U.S. Army, Mills became Service Manger for Packard Motor Car Company. He was elected president of the National Association of Purchasing Agents. Mills was appointed commissioner of purchases and supplies for the City of Detroit in 1926 by Mayor John W. Smith, and became general manager of Detroit Street Railways (DSR) in 1932. One year later he went with Michigan Governor Frank Murphy to direct government enterprises in the Philippines, where he died of typhoid fever on April 6, 1934. (finding aid)
