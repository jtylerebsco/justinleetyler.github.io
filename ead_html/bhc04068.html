Biographical/Historical note

The Fred Hart Williams Genealogical Society was founded in 1979 in Detroit, Michigan. The purpose of the Society is to promote programs and research concerning African-American family history. The Society sponsors educational programs and workshops which explain the techniques of genealogical research most useful for persons of African ancestry. The Society honors the legacy of Fred Hart Williams (1882-1961), a Detroiter who was a pioneer in collecting and interpreting historical materials about African-Americans. Williams used his free time to serve the community as a newspaperman, author, historian, and patron of the arts. The Society also collects, preserves, and makes available to the public manuscripts, documents, genealogical records, and historical materials. Field trips are taken to examine sites and collections of family history records. The Society was the first and remains the only African-American genealogical society in Michigan and one of only two in the Midwest.
