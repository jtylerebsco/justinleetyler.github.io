Biographical/Historical note

The first election for members of the Board of Estimates was held Apr. 7, 1873. Five men were elected from the city at large on a general ticket, to serve for two years. The president of the Common Council, chairman of the Committee on Ways and Means, city comptroller, counselor, presidents of the various boards and commissions, as well as the senior inspector of the Detroit House of Corrections were ex officio members of the board, with the right to participate in its deliberations, but not to vote. The estimates, after being considered by the council, were submitted to the Board of Estimates. The board had the power to reduce, but not to increase, the estimates. By the Act of 1881 the board was abolished, but by Act of June 2, 1887, a Board of Estimates was provided for again.
