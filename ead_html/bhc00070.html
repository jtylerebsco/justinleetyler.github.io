Biographical/Historical note

Born in Strabane, near Belfast, Ireland, in 1739, John Askin came to America during the French & Indian War, serving in the British Army at Ticonderoga in 1758. He came to the Northwest Territory during Pontiac's war. In 1796 he declared his wish to remain a British subject and left Detroit in 1802. He settled in Windsor, Canada, and died there in 1815. He was buried at Assumption, Sandwich, Ontario.,Askin became a fur trader, merchant and loyalist of Albany, New York, Mackinac, Detroit and Windsor.

Scope and Contents note

This collection contains the personal and business papers of John Askin.
