Biographical/Historical note

The Rapid Transit Commission was first appointed by Mayor Couzens in November 1922 to make rapid transit studies. It was not officially established until September 1924 when the voters passed a Charter amendment establishing it. The Commission made five official proposals in the years 1926 through 1933 for subways. In 1933 the voters approved a subway system but no money was appropriated for the Commission or the subway in 1933. Nothing was done until 1944 when the City of Detroit Transportation Board was established. The Rapid Transit Commission was reactivated in 1948 but was deactivated in early 1950 after a rapid transit report was made and hearings end discussions on the report were held. The Commission was reactivated in July 1953 by Mayor Cobo. The Commission remained active until 1967 when the Southeastern Michigan Transit Authority was created by the legislature.

Scope and Contents note

Correspondence, minutes and memorandums of meetings, financial papers, system plans, plans for the James Scott fountain, and printed material.
